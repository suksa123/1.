The Inca Empire fell between 1532 and 1572, primarily due to the Spanish invasion led by Francisco Pizarro, which exploited a massive smallpox epidemic and a violent civil war between brothers Atahualpa and Huáscar. Despite having vastly superior numbers, the Incas were defeated by superior Spanish technology, political maneuvering, and indigenous allies who rebelled against Inca rule.

Key Factors and Events in the Fall:
The Spanish Invasion (1532): In November 1532, Francisco Pizarro arrived in Cajamarca with roughly 168 men, immediately capturing the Emperor Atahualpa during a surprise attack.
Civil War and Disease: Before the Spanish arrived, a devastating epidemic (likely smallpox) wiped out large portions of the population, including Emperor Huayna Capac and his designated heir, causing a power vacuum.
Execution of the Inca: Despite paying a massive ransom in gold and silver, Atahualpa was executed by the Spanish in 1533.
Fall of Cusco: The capital city, Cusco, fell to Spanish control shortly after the emperor's execution.
Resistance and Final Collapse: While a puppet emperor was briefly installed, Manco Inca initiated a prolonged resistance movement. The final collapse occurred in 1572 with the execution of Túpac Amaru I.
Internal Divisions: Many indigenous groups, formerly conquered by the Incas, allied with the Spanish, aiding in the swift collapse of the empire.
