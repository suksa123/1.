The Revolution in Exile: The Collapse of the German Empire ...The German Empire (1871–1918) collapsed at the end of World War I due to military exhaustion, economic collapse, and the November Revolution. Following the Allied "100 Days Offensive" and domestic unrest, Kaiser Wilhelm II abdicated on November 9, 1918, fleeing to the Netherlands. A republic was proclaimed, ending 500 years of Hohenzollern rule, followed by the armistice on November 11, 1918.

Key Factors in the Fall:
Military Defeat: By late 1918, the German army was in retreat on the Western Front, facing dwindling resources and the arrival of fresh American troops.
The November Revolution (1918–19): Triggered by a sailors' mutiny in Kiel, the revolution spread across Germany, forcing the collapse of the imperial government.
Abdication of Kaiser Wilhelm II: On November 9, 1918, Chancellor Max von Baden announced the Kaiser’s abdication without his approval to stem the violence.
The Treaty of Versailles: Post-war, Germany was forced to cede territory (such as Alsace-Lorraine and the Polish Corridor) and dismantle its colonial empire.
